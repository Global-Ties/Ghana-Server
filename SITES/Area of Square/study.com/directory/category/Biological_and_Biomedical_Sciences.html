Biological and biomedical research careers allow someone to be involved in scientific work that will improve human health. Graduate degrees are needed for the most advanced and creative positions in biological sciences.

Inside Biological and Biomedical Sciences

The field of biological and biomedical sciences encompasses the study of biological chemistry, molecular pharmacology, cancer and cell biology, developmental and regenerative biology, genetics, genomics, microbiology and immunology. If you are interested in pursuing a future in the biological and biomedical sciences, you can explore potential degree, distance learning and career options that may suit your goals. Study.com provides a number of resources that can help you achieve your personal and professional aspirations in this field.

Education Information

Degree programs are available at the undergraduate and graduate levels. Undergraduate students can expect to take classes in mathematics for biotechnology, anatomy, chemistry, physics, as well as courses that involve hands-on laboratory work or field research. The types of advanced courses in a graduate degree program depend heavily upon a student's chosen area of focus.

Potential courses include molecular genetics, immunogenetics, animal virology, mechanisms of pathogenesis and pharmacoeconomics. Joint degree programs are also available, such as a joint medical degree (M.D.) with a doctorate degree (Ph.D.) in a field of the biological and biomedical sciences, such as immunology. Employment Outlook and Salary Information

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), biochemists and biophysicists were expected to experience a growth of 19 percent from 2012-2022, which was faster than the average rate of growth for other occupations (www.bls.gov). The BLS also noted that employment of biomedical engineers was predicted to rise at a much-faster-than-average rate, specifically 27 percent over the decade. This growth could be attributed to biotechnological research and development.

Additionally, in May 2013 the BLS noted that biochemists and biophysicists earned a mean annual wage of $91,640. According to data compiled by Payscale.com in April 2014, most biomedical engineers reported earning base salaries from $43,429-$92,399.
