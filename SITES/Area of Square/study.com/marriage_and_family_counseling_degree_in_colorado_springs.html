Marriage and Family Counseling Degrees in Colorado Springs, CO

There is one private non-profit school within 10 miles of Colorado Springs with a marriage and family counseling degree program. Read an overview of the school's program, requirements and admission info and find out if this school is right for you.

Colorado Springs, CO, Marriage and Family Counseling Program

Students who want to pursue marriage and family counseling programs in Colorado Springs have limited public or private non-profit school options. This article provides information about the program available through one 4-year school about 15 minutes from the center of Colorado Springs. Read on to learn about the curriculum, internship and practicum requirements. Check out the table below to learn additional helpful school facts. Also included is information about other educational options.

  • Regis University has a campus situated about seven miles from the center of Colorado Springs that offers a master's degree in marriage and family therapy.

Regis University - Colorado Springs

Regis University's Colorado Springs, Colorado, campus is located on the north side of the city. The campus has 21 classrooms that are equipped with modern technology and state-of-the-art equipment. The university's counseling lab is open to military families in the community who are in need of counseling. This allows students to gain experience helping real people solve family and mental health problems.

Master of Arts in Marriage and Family Therapy

Students completing the master's degree program in marriage and family therapy learn to treat emotional disorders and improve relationships among family members. The school focuses on helping the entire family heal instead of a single individual. Courses cover counseling techniques, methods for counseling military families, ways to counsel teens and pre-teens, treating substance abuse and couples therapy. One course explores the ties between playing and family therapy to determine how it can improve relationships between parents and children. Students complete six credits of practicum work in the counseling lab and complete two semester-long internships in mental health counseling agencies.

Other Schools

There are other schools in the area that offer degree programs in marriage and family therapy, but they are for-profit schools. The University of Phoenix - Southern Colorado is located in downtown Colorado Springs, while Argosy University - Denver, which has both master's and doctoral programs in marriage and family counseling, is about 61.5 miles from the downtown.

One other option exists, but it's about 64.2 miles from Colorado Springs. The University of Colorado - Denver is a public university that offers a Master of Arts in Counseling with a concentration in couple and family counseling. Unlike the other schools mentioned, this program is accredited by the Council for Accreditation of Counseling & Related Educational Programs, which means that the program meets most states' educational eligibility requirements for licensure, certification or other professional credentialing. Once graduates pass the National Counselor Examination, they can earn national certification from the National Board of Certified Counselors without meeting any additional requirements.
