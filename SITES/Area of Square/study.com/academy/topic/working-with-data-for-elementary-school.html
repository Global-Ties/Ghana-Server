About This Chapter

Elementary school students learning to work with data can benefit from reviewing the fun lessons in this chapter. Topics provide in-depth explorations of histograms, column graphs, frequency distribution tables and much more.

Working with Data for Elementary School - Chapter Summary

If your student is preparing to take a quiz or has hit a snag with a homework assignment and needs to better understand how to work with data, this chapter can help! Engaging lessons explore a variety of data concepts to ensure your student understands a Carroll diagram, class interval, frequency histogram, column graph and more. Each lesson features a short quiz your student can take online or on paper to assess their understanding of how to work with data. A more comprehensive chapter exam is also available to gauge his or her knowledge.

Who It's For

Kids who need extra help working with data will benefit from the lessons in this chapter. There's no faster or easier way to learn about working with data.

  • Students who have fallen behind in understanding histograms, frequency distribution tables and other ways to work with data
  • Students with learning disabilities or need a little extra help learning about how to work with data
  • Students who prefer multiple ways of learning math (visual or auditory)
  • Students who have missed class and need to catch up
  • Students who need an efficient and convenient way to learn about working with data
  • Students who struggle to understand their math teachers
  • Students who attend schools without extra math resources

How It Works

  • Find lessons in the chapter that cover working with data topics your student needs to learn or review.
  • Review the lesson with your student or read through the text lesson.
  • Refer to the highlighted vocab words to reinforce learning about working with data.
  • Test your student's understanding of each lesson with short quizzes.
  • Verify your student understands working with data by completing the Working with Data for Elementary School chapter exam.

15 Lessons in Chapter 14: Working with Data for Elementary School

1. Axis Definition: Lesson for Kids

In this lesson, we will define the term 'axis' and talk about the x-axis and y-axis on the Cartesian coordinate plane! We will also go over how to locate any coordinate point on the Cartesian coordinate plane.

2. Box & Whisker Plot: Lesson for Kids

What do you do when you have a lot of numbers that are spread out, and you want to group them into sets and graph them? In this lesson, you will learn how to create and read a box and whisker plot which is perfect to use with that type of data.

3. What is a Carroll Diagram? - Definition & Examples

Have you ever sorted blocks or toys by color or size? In math, sorting things like numbers or shapes helps us to see the relationships between them and to better understand them. In this lesson, we will learn about a really popular way to sort in math.

4. What is a Class Interval? - Definition & Example

This lesson introduces class intervals, a way to divide data into groups. Learn when to use class intervals and how they can be helpful, using the interesting example of the U.S. Census.

5. How to Find a Class Interval

There's a lot of data out there! We often use tables and histograms to help us understand data. In this lesson, you will learn how to find a class interval to make data more useful.

6. What is a Column Graph? - Definition & Example

Normally when you collect data, you have long lists of numbers and information that are not easy to read. Graphs are a perfect way to display this information. In this lesson, we will learn what column graphs are, how they can be used, and how to make one.

7. Frequency Definition: Lesson for Kids

Sometimes you may want to know how often things happen or you want to keep track of how many of one thing or the other you have. That is where frequency and a frequency table will be helpful.

8. How to Make a Frequency Distribution Table

If you are curious as to how to make a frequency distribution table, this is the lesson for you. We will learn what a frequency distribution table can be used for, and how to organize data to include in a frequency distribution table.

9. What is a Frequency Distribution Table? - Definition & Example

In this lesson, we'll discover what a frequency distribution table is and describe its characteristics. We'll also identify some situations where it would be helpful.

10. How to Make a Frequency Histogram

Frequency histograms are a type of graph that allow us to easily compare categories or ranges of data. They are easy to read and construct and can be a great tool for you to display statistics.

11. Frequency Histogram: Definition & Examples

In this lesson, you will discover what a frequency histogram is and how to identify its elements. You will also learn how frequency histograms help organize data through real-life examples.

12. Histogram Lesson for Kids

They say a picture is worth a thousand words. The same is true in math. Graphs are visual pictures of numbers and data, and they can show us what those numbers are saying. This lesson will explain what a histogram is and how it shows a picture of data.

13. Definition of an Ogive Graph in Statistics

An ogive plot is useful in statistics when we want to know how many observations to expect for a range of data. It allows us to quickly see how many observations were measured for all ranges less than a particular number.

14. Ratios Lesson for Kids: Definition & Examples

Just as the English language uses words such as 'taller' or 'nicer' to compare one thing to another, math also has a way to compare different things by using ratios. In this lesson, we will learn what a ratio is, what it looks like and how it helps us with many things that we do.

15. Sets in Math: Definition & Symbols

Do you like solving puzzles and gathering information? If so, then you are going to enjoy working with sets! As we learn all about sets, we will look for things in common and sometimes things that are different. Lets get started!
