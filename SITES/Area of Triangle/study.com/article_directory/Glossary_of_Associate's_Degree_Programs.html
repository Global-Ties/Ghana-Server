Glossary of Associate's Degree Programs

This section contains original articles covering nearly every type of associate's degree program imaginable. In these articles you will find a description of the degree program, an overview of the required coursework, and relevant career information such as job outlook and salary information. Since there are several hundred articles in this section, please select a field of study you are interested in to find a more focused selection of associate degree articles.

Agriculture   ( View 2 Articles )
  • Associate Degree in Animal Management

    Associate programs in animal management prepare students to provide care for, breed, manage and ensure proper housing environments for animals. The program teaches students the importance of conservation, nutrition, training, and more.

  • Associate in Agribusiness: Degree Overview

    Two-year associate's degree programs in agribusiness combine lecture coursework with internships in the agribusiness industry. Students can choose from an Associate of Arts, Associate in Science or Associate of Applied Science degree in agribusiness.

Architecture   ( View 4 Articles )
Architecture   ( View 4 Articles )
  • Associate Degree in Landscaping Design

    The Associate of Applied Science (AAS) degree program in landscaping design is a 2-year program most often available at technical schools or community colleges. Students study landscape design theory and technique, as well as the business aspects of landscape design work.

  • Associate of Arts in Interior Architecture and Design: Degree Overview

    Degree programs in interior design are the A.A. in Interior Studies, A.A. in Interior Architectural Design and A.A. in Interior Design. The programs provide students with a foundation for later pursuing a bachelor's degree in interior design.

  • Associates Degree in Landscape Architecture: Program Information

    Studies in the associate's degree program focus on development of landscaping skills, that include understanding local landscaping codes and regulations, landscaping ecology, and environmental sustainability.

  • Associates in Architecture: Degree Overview and Career Options

    Education in architecture at the associate degree level is commonly available through architectural engineering technology programs. Such programs provide a foundation in the processes of architecture.

Business   ( View 172 Articles )
  • AAS in Accounting Specialist: Degree Overview

    Associate of Applied Science (AAS) in Accounting Specialist programs prepare students to become office personnel who are capable of managing accounting tasks. Learn more about what these technical degree programs entail.

  • AAS in Administrative Support: Degree Overview

    Students who want to study administrative support at the associate's degree level can pursue an Associate of Applied Science (AAS) in Administrative Support Technology. Graduates are prepared to enter the job market or consider further studies.

  • AAS in Business Administration - Finance: Degree Overview

    While finance can be a standalone subject at the associate degree level, it's often offered as a concentration in an Associate of Applied Science (AAS) in Business Administration program as well.

  • AAS in Business Administration: Degree Overview

    An associate's degree in business administration is a great option for those who aspire to work in the business world. These programs provide a foundation for business careers and higher level education.

  • AAS in Business Information Systems: Degree Overview

    Those seeking entry-level technical careers in business settings may pursue an Associate of Applied Science (AAS) in Business Information Systems. Explore this degree to decide if it meets your educational needs.

Communications and Journalism   ( View 37 Articles )
  • AAS in Digital Media Communications: Degree Overview

    Associate's degree programs in digital media communications provide basic training in the field. These programs can prepare students for entry-level positions or bachelor's degree programs.

  • AAS in Television Production: Degree Overview

    Associate of Applied Science (AAS) programs in television production provide training in studio, video and field production techniques and equipment use. Find out about program curriculum and future options for graduates.

  • Associate Degree in Advertising: Program Information

  • Associate Degree in Digital Media: Program Information

    Associate's degree programs in digital media train students in digital video and web editing technology. Students also learn development skills for the various broadcast and multimedia fields.

Computer Sciences   ( View 147 Articles )
  • AA in Computer Information Systems: Degree Overview

    An Associate of Arts in Computer Information Systems (CIS) program provides the networking, programming and troubleshooting training needed for entry-level employment in the IT field.

  • AA in Internetworking Technology: Degree Overview

    Associate of Arts (AA) degrees in internetworking technology, commonly known as computer network technology, aren't common, but there are several related degree programs that provide training in the field.

  • AAS in Computer Applications: Degree Overview

    Associate of Applied Science (AAS) degree programs in computer applications train students in basic computer software installation and hardware maintenance.

  • AAS in Computer Game Design: Degree Overview

    There are several Associate of Applied Science (AAS) programs that train students in computer game design. Explore the curriculum and future possibilities for graduates of these programs.

  • AAS in Computer Information Systems: Degree Overview

    An Associate in Applied Science (AAS) in Computer Information Systems program develops students' abilities to conceptualize, design, and employ information systems. Graduates are prepared for entry-level employment within information technology (IT).

Culinary Arts and Personal Services   ( View 13 Articles )
Culinary Arts and Personal Services   ( View 13 Articles )
  • AAS in Accelerated Culinary Arts: Degree Overview

    An accelerated culinary arts program provides food service training to culinary professionals or career changers. The curriculum may also allow students to gain hands-on experience with their new skills.

  • AAS in Culinary Arts: Degree Program Overview

    Through courses in kitchen operations, menu development and food prep, students in an Associate of Applied Science (AAS) degree program in culinary arts prepare to work professional in restaurants, hotels and other food service establishments.

  • Associate Degree in Cosmetology Business: Program Information

    Associate's degree programs in cosmetology business involve the study of managing organizations and outfits that specialize in professional care for hair, skin and nails. Programs train students to be professional providers of salon services.

  • Associate in Applied Science (AAS): Baking and Pastry Degree Overview

    An Associate in Applied Science (AAS) program in baking and pastry arts can offer aspiring culinary professionals a foundation in pastry preparation, food safety, food presentation and hospitality. Learn more about the programs as well as job options here.

  • Associate in Applied Science (AAS): Culinary Arts Degree Overview

    Associate in Applied Science (AAS) in Culinary Arts programs incorporate training in cooking techniques and food safety with business and management. With experience, graduates can apply for certification through a professional organization.

Education   ( View 12 Articles )
  • Associate Degree Programs for Aspiring Preschool Teachers

    Associate's degrees in early childhood education prepare students to teach preschool. Students learn how to instruct children in reading, writing, science and social studies using play-based activities.

  • Associate Degree in Early Childhood Special Education

    The Associate of Science in Early Childhood Education program is designed for individuals to identify and evaluate the educational and emotional needs of children with disabilities. Read on about the program requirements, careers or continuing options available.

  • Associate Degree in Elementary Education: Program Overview

    Many institutions offer associate's degree programs with a focus in elementary education. In most cases, this type of degree is set up to facilitate transfer into a bachelor's degree program.

  • Associate Degree in Library Science: Program Overview

    Though most librarians need a master's degree in library science before they can find employment, associate's programs are available in the field. These programs introduce students to libraries and information systems.

  • Associate of Early Childhood Education (AECE): Degree Overview

    Students who want to study early childhood education can earn an Associate of Science (AS), Associate of Applied Science (AAS) or Associate of Arts (AA) degree in the field. Find out if these programs meet your educational needs.

  • AAS in Electronic Engineering: Degree Overview

  • AAS in Telecommunications Technology: Degree Overview

  • AS in Electronics Engineering Technology: Degree Overview

    Associate in Science (A.S.) in Electronics Engineering Technology programs train students to work with electronic circuits and devices. Graduates typically seek entry-level employment.

  • Associate Degree in Child Development: Program Summary

    Associate's degree programs in child development focus on various physical, psychological and behavioral aspects of the growth and development of young children. Read on for program requirements, the course curriculum and what to expect after graduation.

  • Associate Degree in Christian Ministry: Program Information

    A student who would like to work in a church might consider an associate degree in Christian ministry. Students develop an understanding of what it means to lead a Christian life and provide Christian-centered service to others.

  • Associate Degree in History: Program Overview and Information

    An Associate of Arts (A.A.) in History degree is available at many community colleges and universities as both online and on campus programs. These 2-year programs typically include courses that cover multiple periods in history.

  • Associate Degree in Home Economics

    Associate degree in home economics teach students how to navigate family and interpersonal dynamics. The programs feature a broad range of courses meant to prepare students for family life or careers.

  • Associate Degree in Interdisciplinary Studies: Program Information

    An associate degree in interdisciplinary studies allows a student to create a unique curriculum that meets his/her future educational and career plans. There is ample opportunity to gain a broad knowledge of the arts and sciences.

  • AAS in Health Information Management: Degree Overview
