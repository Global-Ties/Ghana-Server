Glossary of Bachelor's Degree Programs

This glossary of degree programs contains information about nearly every type of bachelor degree program offered in the United States. In these articles you will find a thorough description of the degree program, an overview of the skills you will attain, as well career and expected salary information. Agriculture   ( View 7 Articles )
  • Animal Science Major Requirements: Info for Prospective Students

    A Bachelor of Science in Animal Science program involves the study of how animals are used for food production. Graduates can enter the agribusiness industry or pursue graduate animal science degrees.

  • Animal Studies Major: Information and Requirements

    A Bachelor of Science in Animal Science is an undergraduate program for students looking to major in animal studies. Students learn about and work with animals through lectures, labs and hands-on field work.

  • Bachelor of Science in Ag Business

    Individuals with a passion for business and an interest in agriculture may enroll in a bachelor's degree program in agricultural business. They emphasize important business concepts such as accounting and management within the agricultural industry.

  • Bachelor of Science in Equine Studies: Program Overview

    Many schools throughout the U.S. offer a Bachelor of Science in Equine Studies, which is an academic field for students with a passion for horses. Topics range from general veterinary and animal science to farming, racing and even equine dentistry.

  • Bachelor's Degree in Animal Science: Program Overview

    Bachelor's degree programs in animal science provide students with a basic understanding of agribusiness, livestock culture, and the preparation of animals for food production purposes. Graduates can find entry-level jobs or pursue more education.

Architecture   ( View 8 Articles )
  • Architectural Technology Bachelors Degree Program Overviews

    Bachelor's degree programs in architectural technology expose students to cutting-edge building construction technology and techniques, giving them the skills and knowledge base needed to excel in the field.

  • Bachelor of Architecture Salary Info: What Do Graduates Earn?

    The salaries of workers who have earned a Bachelor of Architecture degree vary based on a number of factors, such as job function and location. Graduates of this program have the training they need to become licensed professional architects.

  • Bachelor of Architecture: Degree Overview

    The Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch.) is the first professional degree in architecture. Students develop their drawing and design techniques and also learn how to consider the community and environment surrounding their designs.

  • Bachelor of Arts (BA): Architecture Degree Overview

    This pre-professional degree program emphasizes a creative and conceptual approach to architecture. In most programs, students learn about design methods, architectural theories, and applied sciences in building.

  • Bachelor of Environmental Design: Degree Program Options

    Bachelor's degree programs in environmental design allow students to explore the building and design process and add emphasis on environmental impact. Students participate in classroom learning, research and design studio activities.

Biological and Biomedical Sciences   ( View 22 Articles )
  • Bachelor of Biomedical Informatics: Degree Overview

    Bachelor's degree programs in biomedical informatics can provide students with computer skills, as well as medical and biological research capabilities. Learn about program requirements, coursework and job outlook.

  • Bachelor of Science in Animal Behavior: Degree Program Overview

    A bachelor's program in animal behavior is interdisciplinary, meshing studies in biology and psychology to explore animal biology, evolution and animal society structure. Programs typically examine the behavior of aquatic animals, mammals and birds.

  • Bachelor of Science in Bio Medical Technology

    B.S. programs in Biomedical Technology teach students about applied biochemistry, advanced sciences and basic biomedical concepts. There are many opportunities for practical experience in laboratory settings.

  • Bachelor's Degree in Biological Science: Program Information

    Bachelor of Science in Biological Sciences programs cover a wide variety of biology-related subjects, such as cells and molecules, genetics and evolution. Read on for details about concentration areas, the curriculum and job opportunities after graduation.

  • Bachelor's Degree in Biological Therapy

    Schools don't offer a biological therapy bachelor's degree. For students who want to pursue a career in immunotherapy, they can major in immunology, biochemistry or general biology at a four-year university.
