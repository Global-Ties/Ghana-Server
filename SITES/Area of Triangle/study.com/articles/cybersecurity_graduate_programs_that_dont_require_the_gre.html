Cybersecurity Graduate Programs that Don't Require the GRE

There are a variety of graduate cybersecurity programs that do not require applicants to submit their GRE scores in order to be considered for admission. A few of these schools will be mentioned in the article, along with an overview of these programs in general.

The field of cybersecurity offers many interesting career opportunities. Students who would like to work in cybersecurity may be considering pursuing a relevant graduate degree. A number of programs exist that do not require students to take the GRE. This article will discuss some general information about these programs, as well as highlight a few specific schools that fit this description.

Graduate Programs in Cybersecurity that Don't Require the GRE

At the graduate level, programs in cybersecurity that don't require the GRE as an admission prerequisite are most commonly found as master's degrees. These programs are available as both on-campus and online programs, allowing students the flexibility they may need.

Cybersecurity and Information Security Program Info

Generally, master's degrees in cybersecurity require students to complete a core curriculum of courses in addition to elective courses, which may be organized according to a selected concentration area. Many of these graduate programs will require that students take a foundational level course first in order to provide them with a broad understanding of the field of cybersecurity and information security. In this course, students will learn about topics that cover a number of different fields and subjects, like risk management, networking, incident response, and cybersecurity law and ethics. They will also likely learn about how cybersecurity is used in different sectors, from healthcare to the government.

Cybersecurity Policy, Law, and Ethics

It is also common for these programs to include a course that focuses on the policies that are relevant to the cybersecurity field, as well as various legal and ethical issues that professionals working in this field may encounter. The course may cover topics like copyright law, the digital domain, the differences between traditional and digital media, and intellectual property in general. Students will also attempt to solve ethical problems as they pertain to topics in cybersecurity, and will gain a broader understanding of current laws that dictate legal behavior in this field.

Algorithms

Another topic that is covered in cybersecurity graduate programs is algorithms, as understanding the use of algorithms is essential to a successful career in this field. A course that covers algorithms may be introductory in nature, depending on a student's background, and provide them with an overview of how algorithms are designed and used. Other courses may focus on specific types of algorithms like computer algorithms or algorithms that are used in data analytics.

Intrusion Detection

A course in intrusion detection will provide students with the skills and knowledge necessary to identify when various threats are trying to gain access to a computer system. Students will learn about different attack methods and their accompanying identifiers as well as what types of technology are available to help detect threats. In additional to theoretical discussion, this course may also include a hands-on component so that students can put their detection skills to the test.

Computer Forensics

Courses that focus on the topic of computer forensics are generally designed to provide students with an up-to-date look into the modern world of computing and the ways in which computing has affected society. In this course, students will discuss various policy issues in computing, what types of regulations exist in computing, and how the global computing infrastructure is so interconnected. The course may provide students with real world examples and include a hands-on component.

Schools with Cybersecurity Graduate Programs that Don't Require the GRE

  • Fordham University - Fordham University offers a Master of Science degree in Cybersecurity that has an optional GRE requirement. This program has been designated as a National Center for Academic Excellence in Cyber Security Defense Education by the U.S. National Security Agency and the Department of Homeland Security.
  • Johns Hopkins University - Johns Hopkins University also offers a Master of Science in Cybersecurity that can either be completed online, on-site, or through a combination of both on-site and online coursework. The program allows students to pursue one of three tracks: analysis, networks, or systems.
  • Georgia Institute of Technology - At the Georgia Institute of Technology, students can enroll in an online Master of Science in Cybersecurity, which offers tracks in Information Security, Energy Systems, and Policy.

Admission Standards for Graduate Programs in Cybersecurity that Don't Require the GRE

Graduate programs in cybersecurity that do not require applicants to take the GRE and submit their scores still have other requirements for admission that students will want to make sure they fulfill. Some programs may specify that applicants have completed certain coursework during their undergraduate studies, like calculus and computer programming. When applying to these programs, students will need to supply admissions committees with a number of documents in order to be considered. These include letters of recommendation, transcripts from undergraduate studies, a personal statement describing the applicant's motivations for completing the program, and a professional resume or CV.

There are a number of master's degree programs offered by universities around the country in the field of cybersecurity that do not require students take the GRE in order to gain admission, but instead require completion of specific undergraduate courses, letters of recommendation, and more. These programs often feature courses about topics like algorithms and computer forensics.
