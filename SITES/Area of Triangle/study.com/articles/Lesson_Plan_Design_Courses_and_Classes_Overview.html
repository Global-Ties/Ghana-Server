Lesson Plan Design Courses and Classes Overview

Lesson plans contain detailed outlines or descriptions of the classes and are commonly featured in programs related to curriculum design. To learn more about common topics in lesson plan design courses, keep reading.

Start School Search Or view Popular Schools »

Essential Information

Courses specifically in lesson plan design are often found in degree programs focusing on curriculum design, with majors such as Instructional Design or Curriculum & Instruction. The focus of these courses is the conception, planning, and implementation of effective lesson plans.

Here are some common concepts that students in a lesson plan design course might come across:

  • Instructional technology
  • Student assessment
  • Contemporary practices
  • Curricular change
  • Education of diverse populations

List of Common Courses

Introduction to Lesson Planning Course

Designed for teachers, this introductory course gives training in lesson plan writing, analysis and research. Participants are given access to online writing rooms and a writing studio as part of this class. Teaching students also learn to utilize textbooks as instructional tools.

Developing a Lesson Plan Course

This course gives teachers the skills they need to develop lesson plan outlines. The importance of good organizational skills and discipline is emphasized. As part of the lesson planning process, instructors must focus on teaching goals, content, learning materials, objectives, evaluation techniques, and lesson plan rewriting or adjustment.

Secondary School Curriculum Course

Focusing exclusively on secondary education, this course teaches educators how to develop lesson plans for older students. Course topics include the analysis of past plans, limitations of a standard secondary curriculum, the role of the teacher, means of improvement, and organizational issues.

Classroom Management Course

Enrollees learn to evaluate the strengths and intelligence levels of their students in order to develop suitable lesson plans. Proper lesson placement and teaching objectives are emphasized. Teaching students learn to focus on areas such as student placement and classroom arrangement prior to delivering their lessons. Class participants are trained to include independent student activities, such as homework or practice with classmates, into their lesson plans. They are also given tips for motivating students to learn.
