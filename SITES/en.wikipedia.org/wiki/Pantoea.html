Pantoea is a genus of Gram-negative bacteria of the family Enterobacteriaceae, recently separated from the genus Enterobacter. This genus includes at least 20 species.[1] Pantoea bacteria are yellow pigmented,[1] ferment lactose, are motile, and form mucoid colonies.[2] Some species show quorum sensing ability that could drive different gene expression, hence controlling certain physiological activities.[3]

Species[edit]

  • Pantoea agglomerans is the most common Pantoea species recovered from humans and an opportunistic pathogen[4] associated with contaminated catheters and penetrating trauma.[5] It was formerly known as Erwinia herbicola or Enterobacter agglomerans.[6]
  • Pantoea allii[1]
  • Pantoea ananatis[1]
  • Pantoea anthophila[1]
  • Pantoea deleyi[1]
  • Pantoea dispersa[1]
  • Pantoea eucalyptii[1]
  • Pantoea stewartii[1]
